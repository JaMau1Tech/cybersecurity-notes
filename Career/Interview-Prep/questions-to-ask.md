# Questions to Ask the Interviewer

This document contains professional questions that I can ask during interviews for Information Technology and Cybersecurity positions.

---

# General Questions

- What does success look like in this role during the first 90 days?
- What are the biggest challenges someone in this position will face?
- What qualities make someone successful on your team?
- What opportunities are available for professional growth?
- What does onboarding and training look like?

---

# IT Support / Help Desk

- What types of technical issues does the team handle most frequently?
- What operating systems and technologies does the team support?
- What ticketing system does the team use?
- How is success measured for this position?
- Are there opportunities to learn from other IT teams?

---

# Cybersecurity

- What does the Security Operations team look like?
- What security tools does the team use daily?
- What types of incidents are most common?
- How do junior team members continue developing their technical skills?
- What opportunities are available for training and certifications?

---

# Team & Culture

- How would you describe the team culture?
- How do team members typically collaborate?
- What do you enjoy most about working here?
- What is the management style of the team?

---

# Learning & Development

- Does the company support professional certifications?
- Are there opportunities for mentorship?
- What technical skills would you recommend I continue developing?
- How does the company encourage continuous learning?

---

# Closing Questions

- Is there anything about my background that you'd like me to clarify?
- Is there anything that would prevent me from being successful in this role?
- What are the next steps in the hiring process?
- When should I expect to hear back?

---

# Questions to Avoid

Avoid asking questions that are already answered in the job posting or on the company website.

Examples:

- Salary (during an early interview unless the interviewer brings it up)
- Vacation policies (too early in the process)
- Basic company information that could have been researched beforehand
- Questions that suggest a lack of preparation
