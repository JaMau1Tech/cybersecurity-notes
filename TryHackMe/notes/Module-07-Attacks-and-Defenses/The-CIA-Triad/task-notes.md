# Overview

The CIA Triad introduces the three core principles that guide information security:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

These principles form the foundation of nearly every cybersecurity decision and are used to evaluate both attacks and defensive controls.

## Task 2 — Understanding the CIA Triad

### Confidentiality

Purpose

- Prevent unauthorized disclosure
- Restrict access to authorized users

Examples

- Encryption
- Passwords
- Access Control
- Multi-Factor Authentication

Threats

- Data leaks
- Credential theft
- Unauthorized access

---

### Integrity

Purpose

- Prevent unauthorized modification
- Maintain trustworthy information

Examples

- Hashing
- Digital Signatures
- Checksums
- File Verification

Threats

- Data tampering
- Unauthorized edits
- Man-in-the-middle attacks

---

### Availability

Purpose

- Ensure authorized users can access systems

Examples

- Redundancy
- Backups
- Failover
- High Availability

Threats

- DoS
- DDoS
- Hardware failure
- Power outages
