## Overview

This section introduced the many career paths available within cybersecurity and demonstrated that cybersecurity is a broad field with both technical and non-technical roles.

Cybersecurity professionals work to protect systems, investigate incidents, test security controls, and help organizations manage risk.

---

## Learning Objectives

- Understand different cybersecurity career paths
- Learn the responsibilities of various security roles
- Understand the skills required for cybersecurity jobs
- Explore areas of specialization within cybersecurity
- Understand the importance of continuous learning

---

## Key Concepts Learned

### Blue Team

Security professionals responsible for defending systems and responding to threats.

Examples:

- SOC Analyst
- Incident Responder
- Security Engineer

---

### Red Team

Security professionals who simulate attacks to identify weaknesses.

Examples:

- Penetration Tester
- Ethical Hacker
- Red Team Operator

---

### Purple Team

Professionals who work between offensive and defensive teams to improve security.

---

### Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)

Professionals who focus on:

- Security policies
- Risk management
- Compliance requirements
- Security frameworks

---

## Common Cybersecurity Roles

### SOC Analyst

Monitors security events and investigates suspicious activity.

### Penetration Tester

Performs authorized attacks to identify vulnerabilities.

### Security Engineer

Designs and implements security solutions.

### Incident Responder

Investigates and responds to security incidents.

### Security Consultant

Provides security guidance and recommendations to organizations.
